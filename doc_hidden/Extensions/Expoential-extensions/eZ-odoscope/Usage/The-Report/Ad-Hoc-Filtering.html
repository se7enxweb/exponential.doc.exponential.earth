Ad Hoc Filtering

Ad Hoc Filtering

eZ odoscope Ad Hoc Filtering is a functionality that allows you to create specific user groups (segments) or filter existing user groups even further, in a quick and easy way. There is no need for queries and operators, and you can see the results immediately on your report.

As you may know, the Filtergroups and Filters can be manipulated through the odoscope Explorer.

Creating a Usergroup with Ad Hoc Filtering

All you have to do, to create a new usergroup, is select the filters you want to use on your usergroup.

See the examples below:

1) Visitors from Europe and North America:

eZ odoscope Ad Hoc Filtering

This usergroup was created with filters from the same filtergroup, and they are in the same level.

Data:

  • There were 29,701 visitors from Europe and North America, for the period of time selected in The Calendar (always remember: the first filtering level is the Time Period);
    • 22,252 visitors (74.92% of the total amount, 29,701) were from Europe
    •  7,449 visitors (25.08% of the total amount, 29,701) were from North America

 

2) Visitors from Europe and North America that used Google Chrome 9 to access the website:

eZ odoscope Ad Hoc Filtering

This usergroup was created with filters from different filtergroups, and they are in different levels.

Data:

  • There were 18 visitors from Europe and North America, for the period of time selected in The Calendar (always remember: the first filtering level is the Time Period), who used Google Chrome 9 to access the website;
    • 6 visitors (33.33% of the total amount, 18) were from Europe
    •  12 visitors (66.67% of the total amount, 18) were from North America

Note that the all graphics change immediately after you select/deselect a filter.
