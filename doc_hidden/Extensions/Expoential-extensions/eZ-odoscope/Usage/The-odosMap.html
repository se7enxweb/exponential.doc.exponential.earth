The odosMap

The odosMap

The odosMap is a key element of eZ odoscope for traffic flow visualization.

Accessing the odosMap

To access the odosMap, do the following:

  1. On the Dashboard, click on the report you want to analyze
  2. Right click on the report;
  3. Select the options:  Data -> Additional -> odosMap

You will see a screen similar to this:

eZ odoscope odosMap

odosMap Elements

Let's analyze the odosMap elements.

The Rectangle

eZ odoscope odosMap Rectangle

A rectangle represents a single page or a group of pages.  In this case we’re looking at Explore-the-eZ-Market (see URL).

The figures shown have the basis of the current segment   (this can be ‘all sessions’ or a certain segment / user group).

Let’s assume for this example we’re looking at all sessions.

Data analysis:

  • 60,95%   of all sessions have visited this page
  • 109,73sec   average time spent per session on this page (nearly 2 minutes)
  • 1,37 page   impressions (clicks) per session
  • 80,26sec   average time spent per page impression
  • 5,98 %   of all sessions came from outside directly to this page (deeplink, adword, blog, twitter, etc…)
  • 19,67%   of all sessions left the website at this page (exit rate)

Pathways

The pathways are the paths visitors took on your website. Where did they come from? Where did they go?

eZ odoscope odosMap Pathways

The colors signalize the origin of a path:

  • Red coming from eZ fp 2011 going to Explore the eZ Market
  • Mint coming from eZ odoscope going to Explore the eZ Market
  • Yellow from Explore the eZ Market down to the other Extensions

In this scenario we’re looking at a certain type of sessions: They all have been on the eZ odoscope product site (100%).

  • 16,87% came from outside
  • 37,35% left the website at the eZ o product site
  • 10,84% hit the button ‘I want to buy this Ext.’

Features

If you click on the toothed wheel that is at the left side of the odosMap, you will see the following options (buttons) available:

eZ odoscope odosMap Toothed Wheel

Number of Elements Shown (50)

 Click on this button to add more elements and relations (pages and sub-pages) to your report. When you access the odosMap, the default number of elements shown is 50. Now take a look at what happens if you select 10 elements:

eZ odoscope odosMap

You can select from 10 up to 200 elements to visualize, on the odosMap, at the same time.

Magnifier

  The magnifier allows you to magnify the data you see on your report, just like a magnifier does when you look through it:

eZ odoscope odosMap Magnifier

When analyzing a great number of elements (pages and sub-pages), use the magnifier to enlarge the information you see on the screen.

Piktos

 Click on this button to be able to see the average time visitors spent on each page of the website.

eZ odoscope odosMap Piktos

The piktos are the two pie charts shown at the right side of the rectangle.

The pie chart shown on the left (the green, red and yellow one) is a mixture of time spent on average:

  • The yellow area means the page was viewed, on average, for less than 10 seconds
  • The green area means the page was viewed, on average, between 10 seconds and 5 minutes
  • The red area means the page was viewed, on average, for more than 5 minutes

The blue pie chart, shown on the right, represents the distribution of the returners, the visitors who  have visited the web page more than once, when visiting the website; therefore, not unique users.

Fit

  Click on this button to centralize your report (after moving it to the left, right, up or down).

Screenshot

 When you click on this button, you can save a file with an image of the current odosMap.

eZ odoscope odosMap Help

Hierarchy

If you roll the mouse over the area where it says "odosMap", you will be able to see the Hierarchy functionality. It allows you to group web pages. 

eZ odoscope odosMap Hierarchy

When you access this functionality you will see a screen similar to the following one:

eZ odoscope odosMap Hierarchy Page

At the left side you can see the main pages of the website and their sub-pages. At the left side you can see the content of a group (grouped pages).

As you expand or group pages, the odosMap changes accordingly.

You can perform the following actions over the pages hierarchy:

  • Collapse all: Collapses all pages;
  • Expand all: Expands all pages;
  • Level 2: Expands / collapses level 2 pages;
  • Level 3: Expands / collapses level 3 pages.

At the top of the page groups and page area, you can see a filter symbol. It allows you to quickly filter only the page(s) you want. When you click on it, it presents several possible ways you can set the filters to obtain the desired results:
